Our Learning Journey

Exploring the intersection of AI, food discovery, and user experience has been a fascinating journey. Here's what we've learned along the way.

`

Key Learnings

New Technologies

Exploring cutting-edge technologies to bring innovation to meal discovery.

MCP AGENTIC PY Lang AI

We've explored and integrated multiple emerging technologies to power Mealseek:

  • MCP (Model-Context Protocol): Allows us to gather information from the user and google maps
  • AGENTIC AI: We've engaged in extensive discussions about what constitutes a true AI agent versus simple automation, implementing genuine autonomous decision-making in our recommendation engine.
  • PY Lang: Utilizing advanced Python frameworks for natural language processing to understand user preferences in conversational format.

Creating Mealseek pushed our team far beyond our comfort zones:

  • Uncharted Territory: None of our team members had prior experience working with AI systems, requiring rapid upskilling and learning.
  • Learning While Building: We developed expertise in machine learning models and AI integration while simultaneously building our product.
  • Embracing Uncertainty: Working with emerging technologies meant accepting that best practices were still evolving, requiring flexibility and adaptability.

Myth Busting

Dispelling common misconceptions about AI capabilities and implementation.

We've discovered that many common assumptions about AI are misleading:

  • Beyond Chat Interfaces: There's much more to AI agents than simple chat and response mechanisms. Effective AI requires sophisticated backend systems that can understand context, preferences, and provide appropriate recommendations.
  • Prompt Engineering Complexity: Creating effective prompts for AI systems requires much more sophistication than commonly believed, incorporating context, constraints, and domain-specific knowledge.
  • Real Intelligence vs. Simulation: We've discovered the important distinctions between systems that simulate intelligence through pattern matching versus those with genuine reasoning capabilities.

Translating AI capabilities into intuitive user interfaces presented unexpected challenges:

  • Structured Output Challenges: Getting AI systems to produce consistent, structured responses that can populate UI elements required sophisticated response formatting and validation.
  • Balancing Flexibility and Consistency: We developed advanced prompt templates and response parsers to maintain the balance between AI flexibility and UI consistency requirements.
  • Error Handling Evolution: Our approach to error handling and fallback mechanisms evolved significantly as we better understood the complexities of integrating AI responses with traditional UI elements.
